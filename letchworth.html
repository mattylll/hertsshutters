Understanding Letchworth's Garden City Architecture

The world's first Garden City created architectural history, establishing design principles that influenced town planning globally. Our extensive experience working within this unique environment means we understand the specific requirements of each architectural period and building type that makes Letchworth special.

Original Arts & Crafts Garden City Homes

The earliest Garden City houses, built from 1903 onwards around areas like Cheap Street and Sollershott East, represent the purest expression of Garden City ideals. These Arts & Crafts cottages feature distinctive characteristics: roughcast render walls, clay tile roofs, small-paned casement windows, and carefully considered proportions designed to create 'homes fit for heroes' at affordable rents.

These heritage properties require shutters that respect their Arts & Crafts principles while providing modern functionality. We craft shutters using traditional materials and proportions that complement the movement's emphasis on honest construction and natural materials. The small-scale windows common in these cottages are perfect for our solid panel shutters, which provide the substantial, craft-made appearance that aligns with Arts & Crafts philosophy.

Inter-War Garden City Development

The 1920s and 1930s brought substantial expansion to Letchworth, with developments around Norton Way and the eastern areas showcasing evolved Garden City principles. These properties often feature larger windows, bay fronts, and more standardized designs while maintaining the Garden City emphasis on gardens, open space, and quality construction.

The generous window sizes and simplified details of inter-war Garden City homes lend themselves beautifully to plantation shutters. We've installed hundreds throughout these areas, often using white or cream finishes that enhance the clean, modern aesthetic these properties were designed to achieve.

Modernist and Contemporary Developments

Letchworth's continued evolution has seen modernist housing from the 1950s onwards, plus contemporary developments that maintain Garden City principles while embracing new architectural approaches. Properties around areas like Standalone Farm and the newer developments near the Green Belt showcase diverse architectural styles united by the Garden City emphasis on quality, sustainability, and integration with landscape.

These varied property types offer opportunities for our full range of shutter solutions, from minimalist designs for modernist properties to more elaborate configurations for contemporary family homes that reference Garden City traditions.
