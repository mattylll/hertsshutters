Historic Market Town Centre

The ancient heart of Rickmansworth around the High Street and Church Street showcases centuries of continuous prosperity from medieval market town origins through Georgian coaching inn wealth to Victorian railway prosperity. The area contains remarkable buildings from each period, including medieval timber framing, Georgian elegance, and Victorian commercial grandeur that require sensitive treatment to preserve their character.

We specialize in working within the constraints and opportunities these historic buildings present, using traditional materials and installation methods that respect the buildings' heritage while providing modern comfort and functionality. Many of these properties fall within Conservation Areas requiring particular expertise in planning compliance.

Georgian and Victorian Prosperity

The 18th and 19th centuries brought exceptional wealth to Rickmansworth through its position on coaching routes and later railway development. This prosperity funded magnificent Georgian terraces and Victorian villas throughout the town, particularly around areas like Mill End and the better residential streets, featuring the substantial construction and generous window proportions that are perfect for traditional shutters.

These properties often showcase the finest architectural details of their periods, from Georgian mathematical precision to Victorian decorative exuberance. Our shutters for these buildings enhance rather than compete with original features, using proportions and materials that complement the periods' distinctive characteristics.
